VLSI EXPERIMENTS – SEMI-CUSTOM AND FULL-CUSTOM DESIGN

This repository contains VLSI design experiments implemented using both Semi-Custom and Full-Custom design methodologies. The objective is to gain practical knowledge of digital circuit design, ASIC design flow, transistor-level implementation, and physical design while understanding the trade-offs between Power, Performance, and Area (PPA).

Semi-Custom Design

Semi-custom design utilizes pre-designed standard cell libraries and automated design tools to implement digital circuits. This methodology reduces design time, improves productivity, and is widely adopted in modern ASIC development. It provides a balance between design complexity, power consumption, performance, and silicon area.

Full-Custom Design

Full-custom design involves designing circuits at the transistor level, where every transistor and layout structure is manually created and optimized. This approach offers maximum flexibility and control over circuit performance, power, and area, making it suitable for high-performance and memory-intensive applications.

Objective

The purpose of these experiments is to understand the complete VLSI design flow, compare Semi-Custom and Full-Custom methodologies, and analyze their impact on Power, Performance, and Area (PPA). The experiments provide hands-on experience in design, simulation, synthesis, implementation, and verification.

Tools Used

- Cadence Virtuoso – Schematic design, simulation, and full-custom layout implementation.
- Cadence Genus – RTL synthesis and design optimization.
- Cadence Innovus – Physical design, placement, routing, and implementation.
- Verilog HDL – Hardware description and digital circuit modeling.
- Standard Cell Libraries – Semi-custom ASIC design flow.
- CMOS Design Techniques – Transistor-level circuit implementation.
